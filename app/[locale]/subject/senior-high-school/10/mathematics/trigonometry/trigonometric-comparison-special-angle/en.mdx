## What are Special Angles?

Special angles in trigonometric ratios are angles whose ratio values can be determined exactly (precise values) without using a calculator. These values are very important to remember and will be frequently used in various mathematical and physics calculations.

The most common special angles are <InlineMath math="0°" />, <InlineMath math="30°" />, <InlineMath math="45°" />, <InlineMath math="60°" />, and <InlineMath math="90°" />.

## Origin of Exact Values

The exact values for special angles are derived from:

1. **Angles <InlineMath math="30°" /> and <InlineMath math="60°" />**: Obtained from a right triangle with side ratio <InlineMath math="1 : 2 : \sqrt{3}" />.
2. **Angle <InlineMath math="45°" />**: Obtained from an isosceles right triangle with side ratio <InlineMath math="1 : 1 : \sqrt{2}" />.
